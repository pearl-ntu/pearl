Research

Illuminating Molecular Design through Computation and Experiment

Our research focuses on the structure–property relationships of organic dyes. We combine computational modeling, molecular synthesis, and photophysical characterization to establish rational design principles. By integrating theoretical predictions with experimental studies, we investigate and modulate energy transfer, photoinduced processes, and excited-state dynamics, aiming to create next-generation dyes with optimized performance for imaging, sensing, and therapy.

Our Approach

Our research explores the intricate structure-property relationships of organic dyes to enhance their performance in diverse applications such as bioimaging, biosensing, and therapeutic interventions. At the core of our work is computational modeling, which we use independently and in collaboration with synthetic chemists to systematically analyze these relationships and establish rational design principles for specific functional requirements.

By integrating theoretical predictions with experimental data, we derive predictive guidelines that inform the design of dyes optimized for their intended applications. We are also exploring the use of artificial intelligence in chemistry to accelerate the discovery and development of photochemically active molecules, expanding the potential of light-driven processes in science and technology.

Our Goal

Through this comprehensive approach, our group aims to advance the fundamental understanding of dye photophysics while simultaneously enabling practical solutions in imaging, sensing, and therapy.

Research Areas

Computational Modeling

Quantum chemical calculations and molecular dynamics simulations to predict photophysical properties

Molecular Synthesis

Design and synthesis of novel organic dyes with tailored optical properties

Photophysical Characterization

Comprehensive spectroscopic analysis of excited-state processes and energy transfer

AI-Driven Discovery

Machine learning approaches to accelerate molecular design and optimization
